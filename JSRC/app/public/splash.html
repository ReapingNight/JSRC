Just Some Regular Chess
Blind: Yes No

HOW TO PLAY

The player controlling the white pieces is called "White", the player controlling the black pieces is called "Black". White moves first, the players alternate turns. Making a move is required. The game continues until one king is checkmated, a player resigns or a draw is declared. In addition, if the game is being played under time control, players who exceed the time limit lose automatically.
A piece is captured when attacking enemy replaces said piece on its square. This piece is then permanently removed from the game. The king can be put in check, but it cannot be captured.

MOVES

  • The king can move one square horizonatlly, vertically and diagonally.
  • The queen can move any number of empty squares in horizontal, vertical and diagonal direction
  • A rook can move any number of empty squares in horizontal or vertical direction.
  • A bishop can move any number of empty squares in diagonal direction.
  • A knight can move to the closest square not on the same rank, file or diagonal
  • A pawn can move two tiles forward on its first move, and one on every following move.

END OF GAME
The game ends when one of the players successfully manages to capture the opponent's king. This is only possible when the opponent's king is threatened and they have no direct means of averting the threat. This would effectively be checkmate, but in this version of chess, called: "Capture-The-King", the player is required to capture the king, for the game to end.
